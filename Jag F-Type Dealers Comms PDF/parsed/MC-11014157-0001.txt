SYMPTOM:Customers may experience the following:
●Ratcheting, click, or clicking noise in 4WD while turning.
CAUSE: Possible improperly machined front driveshaft

DIAGNOSIS:
If the customer describes the symptom/condition listed above and indicated by proper maintenance records,
perform the Repair Procedure.
REPAIR PROCEDURE:
1. Start the vehicle. With 4WD system in 2WD mode and in a safe area, drive the vehicle between 1-5 MPH
(1-8 KPH) while turning the steering wheel between ¼ turn and full lock.
2. Is there a ratcheting/click noise heard from the front driveline?
● YES>>> This bulletin does not apply. Normal diagnosis should be performed.
● NO>>> Proceed to Step 3 .
3. Place the 4WD system into 4WD high mode.
CAUTION!
Only perform the following diagnosis steps on low traction surfaces (e.g. grass, gravel, snow, or
wet pavement). Performing these steps on dry pavement may produce false results.
NOTE: The noise may be more detectable while turning to the left.
4. In a safe area, drive the vehicle again between 1-5 MPH (1-8 KPH) while turning the steering wheel
between ¼ turn and full lock. Is there a ratcheting/click noise heard from the front driveline?
● YES>>> Proceed to Step 5 .
● NO>>> This Bulletin does not apply. Normal diagnosis should be performed.
5. Replace the front driveshaft, using new driveshaft bolts. Refer to the detailed service procedures listed
in DealerConnect>Service Library under: 03 - Differential and Driveline / Driveshaft / Removal and
Installation.
