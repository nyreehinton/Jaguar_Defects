Poor Fitting Charge Port Door and/or Charge Port Door Sticks Out at Bottom
 
Brand: Model: Model Year: Vehicle Build Date: Engine: Transmission

Chevrolet Blazer EV 2024 2024 SOP April 9, 2024 All All
 
Involved Region or Country North America
Condition
6550658
Some customers may comment on the charge port door not closing all the way and/or the
charge port door sticks out at the bottom.
Cause The cause of the condition may be due to excessive material on the charge port door.
CorrectionRefer to the procedure below to remove some of the material so that the door may close
fully as intended.Subject:

If you encounter a vehicle with the above concern, complete the following:
1. Turn the vehicles wheel to the full left position to make it easier to work around the vehicles charge port door.
2. Position the charge port door to the fully open position.
6550659
3. Remove the Front Fender Ornamentation Vent (1).

4. Using a trim type tool, release the retainers on the charge port door (1) from the housing.
6550660
5. Once the charge door is removed, apply tape to the painted areas surrounding the location in which the fender
ornament was removed, like pictured above.

6550661
6. Sand down all the protruding rectangle slots till they are flush with the surrounding areas like pictured above.
Important: Be careful to not damage the painted areas surrounding the location the ornament is installed.
7. Once the areas are sanded down, reinstall the ornament.
6550663
8. While the charge port door is open, use a suitable file, elongate the existing hole shown on the charge port
housing. Elongate the hole to the red line.

The illustration above shows the amount of material needed to be removed. 1.6 mm on the side illustrated with
the (A) , and 0.25 mm from the two sides (B). The final measurements should be 6.81 mm by 6.6 mm.
Important: Be careful to not damage the painted areas.

9. Reinstall charge port door and verify that it closes as intended.
