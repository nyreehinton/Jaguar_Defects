ConditionSome customers may comment that there is a visible engine oil leak coming from
underneath the middle of the vehicle.
During inspection, some technicians may notice that there is a small oil leak coming from
the engine front cover area.
Cause
6267588
This condition may be caused by improper front cover bolt torque or inadequate sealer
adhesion at one of the six T-Joint locations.
- The oil leak can be found at the T-Joint location where the cylinder head meets the
engine block, or at one of the other T-Joint locations (shown in yellow) in the graphic
above.
CorrectionTo correct this condition, it will be necessary to install new front cover gaskets and follow
the revised RTV sealing procedure as noted below.Subject:

Additional Information
Important: Application of the RTV has changed. Please refer to the picture and steps below for proper RTV appli-
cation.
Page 4 January, 2025 Bulletin No.: 23-NA-020
6002788
Note: The sealing surface must be clean and free of contaminates prior to applying the RTV sealant. Do not touch
the sealing surface once it has been cleaned.
1. Clean the sealing surface at the T-Joints (1 and 2) with low VOC brake cleaner, then wipe dry with a clean lint-
free cloth.
Note: Do not allow RTV to skin over. The engine front cover gaskets and engine front cover must be installed within
8 minutes of RTV application.
2. Apply a 5 mm wide x 15 mm bead of sealant to the 4 T-Joints (1) between the cylinder head-to-engine block and
the engine block-to-lower crankcase.
3. Apply a 5 mm wide x 25 mm bead of sealant to the 2 T-Joints (2) between the cylinder head-to-engine block and
the engine block-to-lower crankcase.
