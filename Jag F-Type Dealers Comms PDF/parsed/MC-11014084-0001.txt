ADA RAMP LOWERING  RETROFIT  
Retrofit Description:  
This procedure removes spacers from the front suspension to allow a lower ride height for proper 
ADA Ramp functionality.

Procedure  
1. Complete the Proterra approved Lockout/Tagout procedure to make the bus safe for work.  
 
2. Using a Schrader Valve tool , remove the four valve cores (shown, at S treetside rear of the 
vehicle) to drain the air from the air bags.  
 
 
3. Using Four Wheel Lifts, raise the bus into the air. Using four Jack Stands, support the bus at 
the jacking points for safety.  Add an additional Jack Stand underneath each side of the front 
suspension to support it when the Wheel Lifts are removed.  
Note: Raising all four corners of the bus will speed the process. But it is possible to do one 
side at a time.

4. Remove the two front Wheel Lifts so tha t the tires may be removed.  
 
5. Using an A ir Impact Tool and a 33mm socket, remove the lug nuts on the front axle and both 
front tires.  
  
 
6. Using  an Air Impact Tool with a 3/4- Inch Impact Socket, remove the four bolts  that attach the 
lower portion of the Streetside air bag to the suspension.  
  
 
7. Remove the spacer.   
 
8. Using an Air Impact Tool with a 3/4- Inch Impact Socket, replace the bolts that were removed 
with four new Half Inch Bolts ( 013377- 001). 
 
9. Using a Calibrated Torque Wrench with a 3/4- Inch Socket, torque the Bolts (013377- 001) to 
40 Foot Pounds.  
 
10. Using Orange Torque Stripe Paint, mark the properly torqued fasteners.  
 
11. Repeat the previous process to remove the spacer from the Curbside of the bus . 
 
12. Using an Impact Tool with a 33mm Socket, replace the tires and lug  nuts.

13. Using a calibrated Torque Wrench with a 33mm Socket, torque the L ug Nuts to 450 Foot

14. Replace the Lug Nut indicators if they were used.  
 
15. Remove the Jack Stands and lower the bus.  
 
16. Using a Schrader Valve Tool, replace the valve cores that were removed earlier.  
 
17. Remove the Lockout/Tagout devices.  
 
18. Power on the bus and allow the air bags to inflate.  
 
19. Verity proper operation of the ADA Ramp before returning the bus to service.
